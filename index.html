Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

The man who led Ghana to Independence

Here is a time line of Dr. Kwame Nkrumah's life:

  • 1909 - Born in Nkroful, Gold Coast (now in Ghana).
  • 1925 - Student teacher in an elementary school run by Catholic mission in Half Assini.
  • 1930 - Obtain a teacher’s certificate from the Prince of Wales’ College at Achimota.
  • 1931 - He was made the headmaster of the school, he started to get involved into politics and founded the Nzima Literary Society.
  • 1939 - Nkrumah completed a Bachelor of Arts degree in Economics and Sociology in Lincoln University, Pennsylvania.
  • 1942 - Earned from University of Pennsylvania a Master of Arts degree in Philosophy and a Master of Science in Education.
  • 1945 - Returned to London and enrolled at the London School of Economics as a PhD candidate in Anthropology.
  • 1946 - Return to Gold Coast to run the United Gold Coast Convention Party.
  • 1949 - He formed the Convention People Party (CPP).
  • 1951 - CPP lead by Nkrumah won the 1951 election and he was released from prison to assume office.

    During his time modern trunk roads were built along the coast and within the interior. The rail system was modernized and expanded. Modern water and sewer systems were installed in most towns, where housing schemes were begun. Construction began on a new harbor at Tema, near Accra, and the existing port, at Takoradi, was expanded. An urgent programme to build and expand schools, from primary to teacher and trade training, was begun. From 1951 to 1956, the number of pupils being educated at the colony's schools rose from 200,000 to 500,000.

  • 1952 - He became the prime minister of Gold coast.
  • 1957 – He led Ghana to independence. Ghana was the first of Britain's African colonies to gain majority-rule independence.
  • 1957 – He got married to Fathia Nkrumah.
  • 1957 – Created a well-funded Ghana News Agency to generate domestic news and disseminate it abroad.
  • 1960 - He became the first president of Ghana.
  • 1961 - Nkrumah initiated the Ghana Nuclear Reactor Project.
  • 1962 - Kwame Nkrumah was awarded the Lenin Peace Prize by the Soviet Union.
  • 1963 - Created the Ghana Atomic Energy Commission.
  • 1966 - Created a hydroelectric power plant, which fueled an aluminum smelter and provided electricity to urban centers and adjacent countries
  • 1972 - He died at the age of 62.
